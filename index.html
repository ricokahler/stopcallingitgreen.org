Stop Calling It Green

How Cheap Power Got Dragged Into The Culture War

A solar panel in a field is a very boring object. It does not roar. It does not smoke. It does not have a tiny American flag decal on the side of it. It does not need a train full of coal, a tanker full of oil, a refinery, a pipeline, a drilling lease, a fuel contract, a protected shipping lane, or a lobbyist explaining why the entire economy will collapse unless the old system gets one more decade of patience. It just sits there. Sunlight hits it. Electrons move. Electricity comes out.

About This Book

This book was written by someone who prefers not to use their full name here. That is not because it is a secret. It is because the book is not about me.

It is self-funded, built mostly from frustration, and exists because I wanted to be able to say I tried. I have been watching and reading people who have already done a lot of this work, and this is my attempt to put the argument in one short, readable place.

There is no donation link, no merch, no foundation, and no plan to turn this into a new organization. The best way to help is to share it with someone who hears "green energy" and checks out before the facts arrive.

The agenda is explicit: I want skeptical Americans to apply their skepticism evenly.

There is a very specific cultural target here, and the book will name it when it earns the right to. The short version is this: I want the biggest skeptical rooms in America to spend a few hours pulling on oil lobbyists, school curriculum, fossil-fuel propaganda, and the money machine behind "normal" energy.

Not because anyone needs to endorse this book. Because if the "follow the money" instinct can point itself at oil and gas with the same intensity it points at everything else, the whole conversation changes.

How To Read This Online

This is an online-first book. Where you see a video box, click or tap through. The point is not just to tell you the research exists. The point is to do some of the research with you.

If you prefer text only, the links are still there. The argument should stand without the videos. The videos just make the machinery harder to unsee.

The plan is simple: publish it as a free GitHub-backed website, make each chapter easy to read and share, and walk away. This is a one-time public note, not a business.

No donations. No merch. No foundation. Share it with someone who hears "green energy" and checks out before the facts arrive.
