Imagine energy politics getting boring.

Not boring because nothing matters. Boring because the lights work, the bills make sense, the grid gets upgraded before it fails, the cheapest useful power gets built, and nobody has to pretend a fuel lobby is a freedom movement.

America has made this harder than it needed to be. We let energy become a culture-war object. We let "energy independence" mean more drilling inside a globally priced fuel system. And we let our skepticism get lazy.

## Keep The Skepticism

The skeptical instinct is good. A free country needs people who ask who profits, who gets the subsidy, and who pays the hidden cost. But suspicion has to be disciplined.

If a government tax credit for a battery makes you angry but a century of fossil fuel support feels like reality, ask why. If a solar farm looks political but a refinery looks neutral, ask why.

You do not have to become "a green person." You do not have to hate oil workers, attack farmers, or put a climate slogan in your bio. You just need to become harder to fool.

When someone says solar uses too much land, ask compared to what. When someone says clean energy is a money grab, ask who funds the oil think tanks. When someone says energy independence, ask independence from what.

## Vote For The Wire

Politically, this means voting for the wire, not the lobbyist.

Look for people who support actual infrastructure: solar, wind, storage, transmission, nuclear where practical, geothermal, hydro, and domestic manufacturing. Look for people who talk about energy as a system, not a costume. People who can say "freedom" and mean less dependence, not more drilling forever.

Oil and gas workers deserve respect. The lobby does not deserve obedience. Skeptical Americans deserve respect. The people monetizing their suspicion do not deserve a blank check.

The United States has the land, sun, wind, engineers, capital markets, and practical restlessness to build a much smarter energy system. It will not be perfect. Infrastructure never is. But it can be better: cheaper, cleaner, more local, and less captured by industries that taught us to confuse their profits with our freedom.

The sun is not a Democrat. A wire is not a sermon. A panel in a field is not a culture war.

It is just energy. It is time we started acting like adults about it.
